How Much Does Radon Mitigation Cost in Cedar Rapids? (2026 Guide)

A complete breakdown of radon mitigation pricing for Cedar Rapids and Linn County homeowners.

March 15, 2026

If your Cedar Rapids home has tested above the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L for radon, the next question is almost always the same: how much will it cost to fix? The good news is that radon mitigation is one of the most affordable home safety improvements you can make, and pricing in Cedar Rapids tends to be lower than the national average.

Average Radon Mitigation Cost in Cedar Rapids

For most homes in the Cedar Rapids and Linn County area, radon mitigation costs between $800 and $1,500. The average installation runs about $1,100 for a standard single-point sub-slab depressurization system. This is the most common system type installed in our area because the majority of Cedar Rapids homes are built on slab-on-grade or basement foundations that respond well to this approach.

Nationally, homeowners pay between $800 and $2,500 for radon mitigation, so Cedar Rapids falls on the lower end of that range. Iowa homes tend to cost less because our soil conditions and common foundation types make standard sub-slab depressurization systems highly effective without requiring complex multi-point setups.

Factors That Affect Radon Mitigation Pricing

No two homes are exactly the same, and several factors can push your cost toward the lower or higher end of the range:

Home Size and Foundation Type

Smaller homes with a single slab foundation are the simplest and least expensive to mitigate. Larger homes, homes with multiple foundation types (such as a partial basement plus a crawl space), or homes with additions built on separate slabs may require additional suction points. Each additional suction point adds $200 to $400 to the total cost.

Radon Levels

Homes with extremely high radon levels (above 10 pCi/L) may need a more powerful fan or multiple suction points to bring levels below 4 pCi/L. Standard installations use a fan rated for typical residential applications, but severely elevated levels sometimes require an upgraded fan, which can add $100 to $300.

System Routing and Aesthetics

The PVC pipe that vents radon from beneath your slab to above your roofline needs a path through or along the outside of your home. Interior routing through closets or utility chases is common and often the most affordable option. If you prefer an exterior installation for aesthetic reasons, or if your home layout requires a longer pipe run, expect slightly higher material and labor costs.

Accessibility and Existing Conditions

Homes with finished basements may require additional work to access the slab for coring. Thick concrete, unusual plumbing layouts beneath the slab, or limited access to the attic or roof can all add time and cost to the installation.

What Is Included in a Typical Installation

A professional radon mitigation installation in Cedar Rapids should include the following:

  • Diagnostic testing to determine optimal suction point location
  • Core drilling through the concrete slab
  • Installation of sub-slab suction pit and PVC piping
  • Radon-rated inline fan with manufacturer warranty
  • Pipe routing from basement to above the roofline
  • System manometer (U-tube) for visual confirmation the system is running
  • Sealing of visible cracks and openings in the slab
  • Post-mitigation radon testing to verify results

Be cautious of any contractor who does not include post-mitigation testing. Verifying that the system actually reduced radon levels below 4 pCi/L is a critical part of the job.

Why Cedar Rapids Homes Often Cost Less

Several local factors work in favor of Cedar Rapids homeowners when it comes to radon mitigation costs. The glacial till soils common throughout Linn County provide good air communication beneath concrete slabs, meaning a single suction point can often depressurize the entire sub-slab area. Many Cedar Rapids homes built from the 1950s onward use straightforward slab-on-grade or full basement construction without complex additions, which keeps installations simple.

The competitive market in eastern Iowa also helps keep prices reasonable. Multiple qualified contractors serve the Cedar Rapids, Marion, and Hiawatha area, so homeowners benefit from competitive pricing.

Return on Investment and Home Value

Radon mitigation is not just a health investment. It directly affects your home's resale value. In Iowa, radon testing is a standard part of nearly every real estate transaction. Homes with elevated radon and no mitigation system often face price negotiations, delayed closings, or lost buyers. A functioning, professionally installed radon mitigation system eliminates this obstacle and can actually increase buyer confidence.

Considering that the average system costs around $1,100 and protects your family from the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, radon mitigation offers one of the highest returns on investment of any home improvement project.

When to Get Multiple Quotes

We always recommend getting at least two or three estimates before committing to a radon mitigation contractor. When comparing quotes, make sure you are comparing the same scope of work. Key questions to ask include:

  • Is post-mitigation testing included in the price?
  • What type and brand of fan will be installed?
  • Does the price include sealing visible slab cracks?
  • Is there a guarantee that levels will be reduced below 4 pCi/L?
  • What is the warranty on labor and the fan?

The lowest price is not always the best value. Look for a licensed and insured contractor with a track record of successful installations in the Cedar Rapids area. Local experience matters because our soil conditions, home construction styles, and building codes are specific to eastern Iowa.
