Professional Radon Solutions for Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a charming college town of approximately 4,500 residents situated about 15 miles east of Cedar Rapids in Linn County, Iowa. Best known as the home of Cornell College — one of Iowa's most prestigious private liberal arts institutions — Mount Vernon blends historic architecture, tree-lined streets, and a vibrant downtown with the surrounding agricultural landscape of eastern Iowa. The town's housing stock ranges from beautifully preserved Victorian-era homes near the college campus to mid-century ranches and newer construction on the outskirts. Regardless of age or style, homes in Mount Vernon share a common vulnerability: the underlying geology of Linn County produces some of the highest radon levels in the nation, and the EPA classifies the entire county as Zone 1 for radon risk.

Mount Vernon's location along the Palisades-Kepler State Park corridor places it atop Devonian limestone formations that are rich in naturally occurring uranium. As this uranium decays through its radioactive chain, it produces radon-222, a colorless and odorless gas that migrates through fractured bedrock and permeable glacial soils into the basements and lower levels of homes. The older homes near Cornell College and along First Street often have stone or block foundations with numerous gaps and cracks that serve as easy entry points for radon. Even the newer homes built along the Highway 30 corridor and in Mount Vernon's expanding residential areas are at risk, as radon can enter through construction joints, utility penetrations, and the natural porosity of poured-concrete foundations.

For Mount Vernon property owners — whether you are a long-time resident, a Cornell College faculty member, or a landlord renting to students — radon testing and mitigation should be a top priority. Rental properties in particular carry a responsibility to provide safe living conditions, and a professionally installed radon mitigation system demonstrates a commitment to tenant health. Our active soil depressurization systems are designed to work efficiently in Mount Vernon's soil conditions, drawing radon from beneath the foundation through a sealed PVC pipe and exhausting it above the roofline where it disperses harmlessly into the atmosphere. The systems operate continuously and quietly, using energy-efficient fans that typically add only a few dollars per month to your electric bill.

We serve all of Mount Vernon and the surrounding eastern Linn County communities including Lisbon, Cedar Rapids, Marion, and Mechanicsville. Our technicians are familiar with the variety of foundation types and construction eras found in Mount Vernon and tailor each installation accordingly. We are licensed and insured, and every project includes a post-mitigation radon test to verify that levels have been reduced below the EPA action level of 4.0 pCi/L. Whether you need a first-time radon test, a new mitigation system, or a fan replacement on an existing system, call us today for a free estimate.
