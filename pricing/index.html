What Affects Your Price

Foundation Type

Basement, crawl space, or slab-on-grade foundations each require different approaches and materials, which affects the overall cost.

Home Size

Larger homes or homes with complex layouts may require additional suction points or longer pipe runs to ensure complete coverage.

Radon Levels

Extremely high radon levels may require a more powerful fan or multiple suction points to bring levels below the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L.

Pipe Routing

The path from the suction point to the exhaust point varies by home. Interior vs. exterior routing and the number of bends affect installation time and cost.

Radon Mitigation Cost Factors in Cedar Rapids

Radon mitigation system costs vary based on your home's construction type, size, and current radon levels. The most common system — sub-slab depressurization for slab or basement homes — typically runs $800 to $1,500 for standard single-family homes in the Cedar Rapids area. Homes with multiple foundation types, crawl spaces, or unusually high radon levels may require more complex systems at higher cost.

Post-mitigation testing is typically included in our installation service. After the system is running for at least 24 hours, we conduct a follow-up test to verify the radon level has been reduced to below 4.0 pCi/L (and ideally below 2.0 pCi/L). If the initial system doesn't achieve target levels, we make adjustments at no additional charge.

We also offer radon fan replacement when existing mitigation systems have failed fans, and system activation for homes with passive radon-resistant construction. All pricing is provided upfront in a written estimate with no hidden fees. Call (319) 777-1776 to schedule your free estimate today.
