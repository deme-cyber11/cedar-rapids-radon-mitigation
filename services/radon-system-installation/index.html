Complete Radon System Installation from Start to Finish

Installing a radon mitigation system is the most effective way to permanently reduce radon levels in your Cedar Rapids home. Our installation team has completed hundreds of systems across Linn County, and every installation is custom-designed based on your home's foundation, soil composition, and structural layout. We do not use one-size-fits-all kits. Instead, each system is engineered to maximize suction, minimize visual impact, and deliver the lowest possible radon levels for your specific property. Most installations are completed in four to six hours, meaning you can have a fully operational radon reduction system by the end of the same day you call us.

Types of Radon Systems We Install

The standard radon mitigation system for Cedar Rapids homes is an active sub-slab depressurization (ASD) system. This involves creating one or more suction points beneath your basement slab, connecting them to a sealed PVC pipe run, and installing an inline fan that operates continuously to draw radon gas from under the foundation and vent it safely above the roofline. For homes with crawl spaces, we install sub-membrane depressurization systems that seal the crawl space floor with a heavy-duty vapor barrier and apply suction beneath it. In homes with both a basement and crawl space, we design combination systems that address all foundation types under a single fan. We also install exterior systems for homeowners who prefer the piping to run along the outside of the home rather than through interior living spaces.

What to Expect on Installation Day

When our crew arrives at your home, we begin with a final walkthrough to confirm the system layout and pipe routing. We then core a four-inch hole through the basement slab at the designated suction point and excavate a small pit beneath the concrete to create an open area for airflow. The PVC pipe is routed from the suction point through the basement, up through the home's interior or exterior, and out above the roofline. The inline radon fan is mounted in the attic, garage, or on an exterior wall, depending on what works best for your property. All penetrations are sealed with fire-rated caulk, and a U-tube manometer is installed on the pipe so you can visually confirm system operation at a glance. The work area is thoroughly cleaned when we finish, and we schedule a post-installation radon test to confirm that levels are below the EPA action level.

Every system we install comes with a written performance guarantee. If post-mitigation testing shows radon levels still at or above 4.0 pCi/L, we will modify and adjust the system at no additional cost until the target is met. Our installations also comply with ASTM E2121 standards for radon mitigation, and all work is performed by licensed and insured technicians. Whether your home was built in the 1920s or 2020s, we have the experience and equipment to install a system that works.
